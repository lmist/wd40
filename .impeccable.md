## Design Context

### Users
People who think in outlines and want visual structure while working on macOS. They use the app to turn indented text into a live mindmap for planning, note-taking, and other structured thinking tasks.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, confident. The interface should feel first-party and trustworthy, with the same quiet clarity as a native Apple productivity app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Cupertino-native minimalism. Flat content surfaces, restrained system-blue accents, Apple typography, and almost invisible chrome. References are Apple’s own desktop productivity tools. Anti-references are flashy dashboards, ornamental gradients, neon glows, and decorative glass cards.

### Design Principles
- Keep content primary and chrome secondary.
- Use Apple-native rhythm: system colors, 4pt spacing, and restrained motion.
- Favor structural clarity over decorative flourish.
- Make key actions obvious without making the window feel busy.
- Optional presentation modes can be expressive, but they must stay typographically disciplined and reversible.
